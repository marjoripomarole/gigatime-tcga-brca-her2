## Current Priority

The current research priority is not another classifier score. It is reducing ambiguity around whether the HER2-low versus HER2-zero signal reflects tumor biology, tissue composition, or TCGA acquisition/source-site structure.

The 2026-06-04 generic-embedding controls sharpened this: H-Optimus-0 and Virchow2 embeddings with no immune interpretation reproduce the low-versus-zero separation and the same source-site collapse as GigaTIME. The remaining priority is therefore external/site-balanced validation and pathologist tumor-region review, not more GigaTIME-internal sensitivity analyses.

BCNB is now the immediate external-validation path. Its full clinical file confirms preserved HER2 IHC 0/1+/2+/3+ scoring, yielding 127 HER2-zero and 654 HER2-low cases from one scanner, with grade/ER/PR/Ki67/molecular subtype available for covariate control. The patch-input gate and first paired foundation-model pilots are now complete.

Patch-input gate update: `paper_patches.zip` is local and valid, with 76,578 256x256 RGB patches mapping cleanly to all 1,058 patients (`docs/bcnb_paper_patches_audit.md`). Full and capped-10 patch manifests are also local under ignored `data/bcnb/`. The H-Optimus-0 and Virchow2 hash-capped patch pilots plus paired comparison agree: the external signal is real enough to beat shuffled labels, but modest, highly concordant across encoders, and comparable to clinical covariates. Sampling, clinical-stratified, and score-driver checks do not overturn that; performance is uneven across grade/receptor/subtype slices and residual signal after measured clinical + patch-QC adjustment remains weak. The next scientific step is multi-seed patch-sampling sensitivity or full WSI processing only if the paper needs stronger slide/tissue-area controls.
